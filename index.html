Questions

Common questions from lawyers and notaries

Does a lawyer or notary in Belgium actually need a website?

Increasingly, yes. Most people now search online before calling a lawyer or notary, even when the first contact still happens by referral or phone. A firm with no site, or an outdated one, is invisible at exactly the moment someone is deciding who to call.

Are lawyers and notaries allowed to advertise online in Belgium?

Yes, within limits set by their own professional body — the Ordre des barreaux (OBFG/OVB) for lawyers, and Fednot for notaries. Each has its own guidelines on professional advertising. We design within those norms — clear, factual, credential-focused content — but always recommend confirming specifics with your own professional body before publishing.

Is a notary's website different from a lawyer's?

Yes. A notary holds a public office with statutory functions, so the site needs to reflect that formal role and the specific acts a notary handles. A lawyer's site is closer to a private practice, often built around specializations. We build each around what actually applies to that profession, not a copy-pasted template.

How much does a website cost for a law firm or notary office?

It depends on scope — a site alone costs less than a site plus branding plus local SEO. We give a fixed quote after a short call about your practice, not a generic price list, since a one-lawyer practice and a multi-partner firm need different things.

How long does it take to build?

Most sites are live within a few weeks of kickoff. Branding work (logo, visual identity) adds time upfront but doesn't have to delay the site — we can sequence them in parallel depending on what you need first.
